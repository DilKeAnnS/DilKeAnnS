🇮🇳 BHARAT – Know Your Country
📜 Constitution of India
Adopted: 26 November 1949 | Effective: 26 January 1950
Length: Longest written constitution in the world (448 Articles, 25 Parts, 12 Schedules)
Preamble: "WE, THE PEOPLE OF INDIA... give to ourselves this Constitution"
🇮🇳 Fundamental Rights (Articles 12-35)
• Right to Equality (Article 14-18)
• Right to Freedom (Article 19-22)
• Right against Exploitation (Article 23-24)
• Right to Freedom of Religion (Article 25-28)
• Cultural and Educational Rights (Article 29-30)
• Right to Constitutional Remedies (Article 32)
🇮🇳 Fundamental Duties (Article 51A)
• Respect National Flag and Anthem
• Protect environment, public property
• Pay taxes honestly
• Vote in elections
⚖️ Important IPC Sections (Indian Penal Code)
Section 302: Punishment for Murder – Life Imprisonment or Death
Section 376: Punishment for Rape – 10 years to Life Imprisonment
Section 498A: Dowry Harassment – Up to 3 years imprisonment
Section 354: Outraging Women's Modesty – 1 to 5 years imprisonment
Section 420: Cheating – Up to 7 years imprisonment + Fine
Section 509: Word/gesture insulting modesty of woman
Section 124A: Sedition (controversial, being reviewed)
Section 153A: Promoting enmity between groups
Section 295A: Insulting religious beliefs
Section 323: Voluntarily causing hurt – Up to 1 year imprisonment
🛡️ Legal Rights Every Citizen Must Know
RTI (Right to Information) Act 2005: You can ask any government department for information. Reply within 30 days.
RTE (Right to Education) Act 2009: Free compulsory education for children 6-14 years
Right to Legal Aid (Article 39A): Free lawyer for those who cannot afford
Right to be arrested with warrant: Police must show warrant (except in specific cases)
Right to know arrest grounds (Article 22): Police must inform reason for arrest immediately
👮 How to Deal with Police
• Always ask for warrant before allowing home search
• You have right to one phone call after arrest
• You have right to lawyer presence during interrogation
• Police cannot torture or beat you (Article 21 – Right to Life)
• FIR process: Zero FIR can be filed in any police station
• If police refuses FIR, approach Superintendent of Police (SP) or court
📝 How to File Complaint Against Police
• Write to Superintendent of Police (SP) of the district
• Approach State Human Rights Commission
• File complaint in Magistrate Court under Section 156(3) CrPC

⚖️ Judge – Salary & Expense Breakdown
Supreme Court Judge: ₹2,80,000 per month
Chief Justice of India (CJI): ₹2,80,000 per month (special allowances)
High Court Judge: ₹2,25,000 per month
District Judge: ₹1,50,000 - 1,80,000 per month
💰 Perks & Allowances
• Free government bungalow (type VI to VIII for SC/HC)
• Free electricity, water, phone, internet
• Free vehicle + driver
• Free medical for self + family
• Free domestic travel (air/train 1st class)
• Staff (secretary, clerks, peons) – fully paid by govt
• Security personnel (2-4)
Pension: 50% of last drawn salary + allowances (for life)
Post-retirement: Free housing, vehicle, medical for lifetime

📋 How to Deal with Public Servants
If asked for bribe (Government officer): Record conversation, note name/designation, file complaint with State Anti-Corruption Bureau or CBI
If file is stuck: File RTI (Right to Information) – response within 30 days mandatory
If service not provided: Write to Public Grievance Portal (CPGRAMS) – govt. grievance system
If police officer misbehaves: Note badge number, record incident, file complaint with Superintendent of Police (SP)
